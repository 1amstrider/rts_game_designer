# Kingdom Design: The Seven Factions

## Philosophy

Each kingdom represents a distinct **race-based faction** with unique visual identity, mechanical themes, and playstyle. All kingdoms progress through the same Ages, but their interpretation of each era differs thematically.

---

## 1. Humans

**Status:** Baseline / Balanced
**Visual Theme:** Medieval European, Roman, Napoleonic (Age-dependent)

### Design Principles
- Jack-of-all-trades, no extreme strengths or weaknesses
- Strong organization and formation bonuses
- Technology-focused: humans advance through ages most efficiently
- Heroes tend to be leaders/buffing types

### Example Heroes
- **Knight Captain** (Melee Tank + Aura Buffs)
- **Royal Archer** (Ranged DPS)
- **Field Marshal** (Strategic Buffs, Formation Abilities)

---

## 2. Kingdom of Ores (Dwarves)

**Visual Theme:** Mountain holds, runic magic, heavy metal, beards, steam/mechanical contraptions in later ages

### Design Principles
- Highest armor and HP in the game
- Slow movement speed
- Superior siege and defensive capabilities
- Runes provide passive buffs and resistances
- Masters of technology and craftsmanship

### Racial Traits
- +30% Armor
- -20% Movement Speed
- Can build defensive structures faster
- Units cost more but are more durable

### Example Heroes
- **Iron Lord** (Ultra-heavy tank with runic shields)
- **Runesmith** (Support: applies buffs/debuffs through runes)
- **Slayer** (Berserker-type, high damage but low defense)

---

## 3. Kingdom of Magic (Elves + Wizards + Gnomes)

**Visual Theme:** Forests, crystalline structures, glowing magic, nature spirits, arcane laboratories

### Design Principles
- Highest magic damage and magic defense
- Lowest physical armor and HP
- Excellent ranged units and spellcasters
- Nature magic (Elves) vs Arcane magic (Wizards) vs Gadgets (Gnomes)

### Sub-factions
| Sub-faction | Theme | Strength |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| **Wood Elves** | Nature, forest spirits | Nature magic, camouflage, ranged |
| **High Elves** | Arcane, crystalline | Pure magic damage, shields |
| **Gnomes** | Alchemy, gadgets | Mechanical contraptions, explosives |

### Example Heroes
- **Arcanist** (Pure magic DPS, shield breaker)
- **Forest Warden** (Nature control, roots, summons)
- **Gnome Tinkerer** (Deploys gadgets, mines, turrets)

---

## 4. Kingdom of Evil (Necromancers + Witches + Goblins)

**Visual Theme:** Dark fortresses, bones, shadows, green fire, swarms, undead

### Design Principles
- Swarm tactics: weak individual units but summoned in numbers
- Necromancy: raise dead enemies as allies
- Curses and debuffs are primary tools
- Sacrifice mechanics: hurt your own units for powerful effects

### Sub-factions
| Sub-faction | Theme | Strength |
|-------------|-------|----------|
| **Necromancers** | Undead, death magic | Raise dead, drain life, fear |
| **Witches** | Curses, poisons | Debuffs, area denial, transformations |
| **Goblins** | Swarms, explosives | Cheap, numerous, kamikaze units |

### Example Heroes
- **Lich Lord** (Undead summoner, death aura)
- **Blight Witch** (Poison/curses, area denial)
- **Goblin Warboss** (Swarm buffs, kamikaze buffs)

---

## 5. Kingdom of the Ancients (Silurian + Dinosaurs)

**Visual Theme:** Jungle, primitive stone tools, dinosaurs, beast riding, tribal markings

### Design Principles
- Primitive but powerful
- No magic, no advanced armor
- Dinosaur cavalry is unmatched in charge damage
- Beast synergy: heroes buff nearby dinosaur/beast units

### Racial Traits
- No magic damage, no magic defense
- +50% Charge damage
- Units are cheap but technologically limited
- Cannot advance past certain tech in later ages without external help

### Example Heroes
- **Dino-Rider Chieftain** (Heavy cavalry on T-Rex mount)
- **Beast Shaman** (Buffs beasts, summons animal spirits)
- **Tribal Warmaster** (Melee berserker, rallying cry)

---

## 6. Kingdom of the Beasts (Minotaurs, Trolls, Balrogs, Dragons, Serpents)

**Visual Theme:** Dark caves, volcanic areas, massive monsters, primal fury

### Design Principles
- Individual units are the strongest in the game
- Very expensive, very low unit counts
- Terror mechanics: enemy units may panic
- Regeneration on most units
- No ranged units (except dragons)

### Racial Traits
- +100% HP compared to human equivalents
- Units cost 3x as much
- Maximum army size is halved
- Regenerate 1% HP per second out of combat

### Example Heroes
- **Minotaur King** (Melee terror, charge through lines)
- **Dragon Ancient** (Flying, fire breath, terror)
- **Balrog Lord** (Fire aura, whip, massive AOE)
- **Serpent Queen** (Poison, constrict, water bonuses)

---

## 7. Kingdom of the Divines (Gods, Angels, Demigods, Oracles, Archangels)

**Visual Theme:** Golden light, clouds, divine architecture, halos, wings

### Design Principles
- Most powerful individual units
- Extremely expensive
- Holy damage is effective against undead and demons
- Healing and resurrection abilities
- Limited numbers: divine units are rare

### Racial Traits
- Holy damage bonus vs Evil kingdom
- Self-healing and ally-healing
- Can resurrect fallen allies (cooldown limited)
- Units cost 5x human equivalents
- Maximum 3 divine heroes per army

### Example Heroes
- **Paladin** (Heavy tank, holy damage, divine shield)
- **Archangel** (Flying, sword of light, resurrection)
- **Oracle** (Support: prophecy buffs, reveal map, counter-spells)
- **Demigod** (Melee/Range hybrid, extremely powerful, game-winning unit)
